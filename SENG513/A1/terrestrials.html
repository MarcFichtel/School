Marc's Tarantula Collection

SENG 513 Assignment 1

My Terrestrial Tarantulas


Kass
Brachypelma albiceps
Mexican Golden Redrump

  • Kass is about 1/2 inch, is a very calm T and has never given me a threat posture or kicked hair at me.
  • Does occasionally flip over the water bowl for no good reason.
  • Never hides and doesn't move much most of the time.
  • Enjoys prekilled crickets very much.
  • Doesn't web much.
  • Got him from Tangled in Webs via mail in November 2017.

Majora
Brachypelma albopilosum
Honduran Curlyhair

  • Majora was an early addition purchased from Tarantula Canada at the YYC Reptile Expo in September 2017.
  • This T has been digging like crazy since the moment she was brought home.
  • Seriously, her cave rivals those of any fossorial T I own.
  • Lazily waits until crickets walk straight into her burrow.
  • Is about an inch large, and has webbed a little bit to stabilize her cave.

Biggy Smalls
Cyriocosmus elegans
Trinidad Dwarf Tiger

  • Got this T at the delicate size of 1/8 inch in November 2017.
  • Has grown considerably since then.
  • Despite his small size, he already has his adult coloration
  • Heart butt!
  • Has eaten prekilled crickets twice his size.
  • Likes to use cricket carcasses as burrow roofs.

Mipha
Euathlus sp. Red
Chilean Flame

  • Got this confirmed female from Tarantula Canada in December 2017.
  • Pretty sure her and Ruto are from the same egg sac.
  • Slightly larger and more colorful than her sister.
  • Likes to explore outside her enclosure whenever it is opened, often leading to unplanned handling sessions.
  • Moves very slowly - least skittish T on here.
  • Has been fasting since she arrived, barely even touching her food.

Ruto
Euathlus sp. Red
Chilean Flame

  • Got this confirmed female from Tarantula Canada in December 2017.
  • Pretty sure her and Mipha are from the same egg sac.
  • Together with her sister the most docile T ever.
  • Likes to explore outside her enclosure whenever it is opened, often leading to unplanned handling sessions.
  • Moves very slowly - least skittish T on here.
  • Only slightly better with food than her sister.

Pennywise
Grammostola pulchra
Brazilian Black

  • This confirmed male was the beginning of the proverbial slippery slope.
  • Searching for a female G pulchra for when this guy matures.
  • Look at that beautiful velvet black color!
  • No more than 2 weeks after this one was purchased, he decided to bury himself for 3 months to molt...
  • Is very calm and docile, but also a quick and merciless hunter of crickets and wax worms.

Revali
Monocentropus balfouri
Socotra Island Blue Baboon

  • This T is about 1/2 inch lagre, and was acquired in October 2017 from Tarantula Canada at the YYC Reptile Expo.
  • Will look absolutely stunning with his adult coloration.
  • Has the most intricate design for burrows.
  • Will dig out a basement and establish both a first and second floor with webs.
  • ONLY eats prekilled prey at the moment (pickiest eater of them all).
  • Has been pretty calm, but skittish up until now.

Dinraal
Nhandu chromatus
Brazilian Red

  • Probably the shyest T here.
  • Spends most of his time burried away without any windows to check on him.
  • Good eater when he's out, but prefers prekilled prey.
  • Likes it dryer and warmer than most other T's.
  • Generally calm and doesn't move much.
  • Bought this T in November 2017 from Tangled in Webs via mail.

Tetra
Nhandu tripepii
Brazilian Giant Blonde

  • Tetra was shipped with the same order as her cousin Dinraal.
  • Slightly larger than Dinraal at about 2/3 inch.
  • Likes to play with her prey and then not eat it.
  • Gets scared easily by live prey.
  • Despite its common name, this T's adult size is about the same as most other T's at about 7 inches.
  • Will be a big blonde fuzzy Teddybear as an adult.

Charlotte
Theraposa stirmi
Burgundy Goliath Birdeater

  • Not called "Goliath" for no reason at an adult size of 11-12 inches!
  • Urticating hairs of DOOM.
  • Unquenchable appetite for anything that moves.
  • Murders multiple large crickets with enthusiasm.
  • The latest addition, got this T in late December 2017 from Pisces.
  • Actively tries to escape her enclosure on a weekly basis.
